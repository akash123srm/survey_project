{% bootstrap_field form.time_constraint %}

Hard Constraint - If the information need is addressed at a specific time. For instance if a user needs to get an idea for a good Christmas present, then the information need is clearly addressed at a specific time with the urgency depending on the current date.

Soft Constraint - If the information need is addressed at a vague time. For instance if the information need is of the kind:"Any ideas for summer holidays?".

Independent - Not dependent on the time. For instance if the information need is of the kind: "What is your favourite football team?".

{% bootstrap_field form.answer_validity %}

Long - Information need which is valid for a very long time (e.g. decades, centuries, forever).For instance, "When was Mozart born?" - the answer is valid forever.

Medium - Information need which is valid for a long time (e.g. months, maybe years). For instance, "Who is the current football player of the year?" or "How much does the new MacBook Pro cost?"

Short - Information need which is valid for a short time (e.g. hours, days, maybe weeks). For instance, "Will it be sunny tomorrow?"

{% bootstrap_field form.generality_applicability %}

High Generality - This includes all the information needs that are not tailored to one particular user. So the information need is shared by a lot of people, for instance: "What is the average cost of living in Munich?"

Medium Generality - This covers all the information needs that are needed to satisfy a specific problem that targets a specific subset of people, for instance: "How to compute running time in Java?". In this case the given information need targets programmers.

Low Generality - This covers all the information needs that are needed to satisfy a specific problem that a particular user has, for instance: "My GRE score is 310. Is this sufficient to get admission in a good university in the United States?". In this case the information need is related to only the specific user who has a GRE score of 310.

{% bootstrap_field form.location_constraint %}

High - It signifies that user's location has a definite impact on information need & the type of answer expected, for instance: "Where is the closest ALDI supermarket to Klinikum Grosshadern U-Bahn Station?"

Low - It signifies that location does not impact the information need, for instance: "Which is the best Android phone in the market at present?"

{% bootstrap_field form.degree_knowledge %}

High - Knowledge that is needed to satisfy an information need is codified i.e. it is generally defined in form of facts (books or articles), for instance: "What are the symptoms of Parkinsonâ€™s disease?"

Low - Knowledge that is needed is not codified i.e. it is widely discussed & controversial, for instance questions that are asking for recommendation (e.g. "What is the best restaurant in Munich?") or opinions (e.g. "Do you like the new BMW i3?")
