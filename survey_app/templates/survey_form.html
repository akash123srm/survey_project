{% bootstrap_field form.time_constraint %}

Hard Constraint - The information need needs to be addressed at a specific point in time, e.g. a user needs to get an idea for a good Christmas present, then the information need is clearly addressed at a specific time with the urgency depending on the current date.

Soft Constraint - The information need is addressed at a vague time, e.g. the information need is of the kind: "Any ideas for summer holidays?".

Independent - Not dependent on the time, e.g. the information need is of the kind: "What is your favorite football team?".

{% bootstrap_field form.answer_validity %}

Long - Reply to information need is valid for a very long time (e.g. decades, centuries, forever) , e.g. "When was Mozart born?" - the answer is valid forever.

Medium - Reply to information need is valid for a long time (e.g. months, maybe years), e.g. "Who is the current football player of the year?" or "How much does the new MacBook Pro cost?"

Short - Reply to information need is valid for a short time (e.g. hours, days, maybe weeks). For instance, "Will it be sunny tomorrow?"

{% bootstrap_field form.generality_applicability %}

High Generality - Information needs that are not tailored to one particular user; information need is shared by a lot of people, for instance: "What is the average cost of living in Munich?"

Medium Generality - Information needs that are important for a specific subset of people, for instance: "How to compute running time in Java?". In this case the given information need targets programmers.

Low Generality - Information needs that are only important for a particular user, for instance: "My GRE score is 310. Is this sufficient to get admission in a good university in the United States?". In this case the information need is related only to the specific user who has a GRE score of 310.

{% bootstrap_field form.location_constraint %}

High - The user's physical location has a definite impact on the information need and the type of answer expected, e.g.: "Where is the closest ALDI supermarket to Klinikum Grosshadern metro station in Munich, Germany?"

Low - The user’s location does not impact the information need, for instance: "Which is the best Android phone in the market at present?"

{% bootstrap_field form.degree_knowledge %}

High - Knowledge to satisfy the information need is codified i.e. it is defined in form of facts (books or articles) and there is a common agreement, e.g.: "What are the symptoms of Parkinson’s disease?"

Low - Knowledge to satisfy the information need is not codified i.e. it is widely discussed and controversial, e.g. questions asking for recommendation (e.g. "What is the best restaurant in Munich?") or opinions (e.g. "Do you like the new BMW i3?")
